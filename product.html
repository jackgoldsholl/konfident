Product

A purchase-confidence signal, synthesized from the reviews you already have.

Konfident connects to your store, reads the reviews already sitting in your review app, and synthesizes them per product into a confidence signal that appears right on the product page—at the exact moment a shopper is deciding whether to buy.

Shoppers already lean on reviews to decide. The Baymard Institute found that 95% of users relied on reviews in large-scale product-page UX testing, and Yotpo reports that shoppers who engage with reviews and photos are roughly 161% more likely to convert. The problem is that the signal is scattered across dozens or hundreds of individual reviews. Konfident does the reading and interpreting for the shopper, per SKU, so the decision gets easier instead of harder.

How it works

Three steps, and the heavy lifting is Konfident's.

🔌

1. Connect your store and existing reviews

Install on Shopify and point Konfident at the reviews you already collect—whether they live in Yotpo, Judge.me, Okendo, Stamped, Loox, or another supported app. There is nothing to migrate and nothing to re-collect. Your review platform stays exactly where it is.

🧠

2. Konfident synthesizes a per-SKU signal

For each product, Konfident reads the full body of review content and ratings and synthesizes them into a purchase-confidence signal: what a product is actually for, what to expect, and where the trade-offs are. Every product gets its own signal—there is no store-wide average standing in for the specifics.

🛍️

3. The widget shows it on the product page

The synthesized signal renders in a lightweight widget on the product page, where the shopper is making the decision. Instead of leaving them to excavate a wall of reviews on their own, Konfident surfaces the interpretation at the point of purchase.

What makes it different

Most review tools summarize—they compress a pile of reviews into a shorter pile of reviews. Konfident synthesizes. The distinction matters: a summary tells you what people said on average, while a synthesis interprets what the reviews mean for the specific decision a shopper is trying to make right now. "Is this right for me, specifically?" is a different question from "is this good in general," and it is the one that determines whether an add-to-cart becomes a keeper or a return.

Konfident is built around that decision moment. It reads for signal a shopper would otherwise have to reconstruct by hand—fit, expectations, who the product suits, where it falls short—and presents it as an interpretation rather than a digest. We wrote about why this line matters in AI review synthesis vs. summarization.

Signal modes

You control the posture of the signal. Konfident offers three modes, so the confidence signal matches your brand's voice and your category's honesty norms.

➕

Positive-only

Surfaces the strengths and best-fit use cases that the reviews consistently support. Useful for categories where the primary job is reinforcing genuine strengths a shopper might otherwise miss.

⚖️

Balanced

Leads with strengths but names the honest trade-offs alongside them. This is the default posture for merchants who want confidence built on candor rather than cheerleading.

🔎

Full-spectrum

Presents the complete picture—who a product is for, who it isn't, and the caveats worth knowing before buying. Best suited to considered purchases where a mismatch is expensive for both sides.

🙈

Hidden when the signal is thin

When a product has too few reviews to synthesize an honest signal, the widget stays hidden rather than manufacturing false confidence. Konfident would rather show nothing than show something it can't stand behind.

Built for performance and privacy

Konfident is designed to be a good citizen on your product page and a conservative handler of data. It reads only public review data—the reviews and ratings already displayed on your store—plus product metadata. It does not collect or store shopper PII.

🔒

No shopper PII

Konfident reads public review content and product metadata—nothing about the individual shopper viewing the page. There is no browsing identity, no payment data, and no personal profile involved in generating a signal.

⚡

Async and cached

Synthesis happens asynchronously and the resulting signal is cached per SKU, so the widget serves a precomputed signal on the product page rather than doing expensive work in the shopper's critical rendering path.

🛡️

Compliant by design

Because Konfident works from public review data and avoids shopper PII, it is GDPR compliant by design and CCPA ready. See the Data Hub for exactly what we read, use, and never store, and Security for our posture.

📄

Transparent about limits

We are precise about what Konfident does and does not do. The signal is an interpretation of your reviews, not a guarantee, and the widget stays honest—including staying hidden when the underlying review signal is too thin to synthesize.
