Privacy Policy

How Konfident collects, uses, and protects information. We read a store's public review data, store no shopper personal information, and build for privacy from day one.

⚠️ Early-stage template notice. This Privacy Policy is an early-stage template provided for transparency during Konfident's private beta. It has not yet been reviewed by counsel and must be reviewed and approved by a qualified attorney before it is relied upon for commercial use in your jurisdiction. It does not constitute legal advice.
Last updated: July 1, 2026

1. Introduction

Konfident ("Konfident," "we," "us," or "our") provides a service that reads a Shopify (or other supported commerce platform) store's existing product reviews and synthesizes a per-product "purchase confidence" signal for display on product pages. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect from and about the merchants who use our service, how we use it, who we share it with, and the choices and rights you have.

Konfident is currently pre-launch and operating in private beta. We designed the service to be data-minimal from the start: we read only publicly available review content, we do not collect or store the personal information of a store's shoppers, and, on our Pro plan, we anonymize return-outcome data before it is ever aggregated. We consider ourselves GDPR compliant by design and CCPA ready. This policy applies to our marketing website, our merchant dashboard, and the Konfident application when installed on a store.

If you do not agree with this policy, please do not use the service. Your continued use of Konfident constitutes acceptance of the practices described here.

2. Information We Collect

We collect a deliberately narrow set of information, described below by category.

Merchant account information

When you sign up for early access or create an account, we collect information you provide directly, such as your name, business/store name, email address, store URL, the commerce platform you use, and — if you subscribe to a paid plan — billing details processed through our payment provider. We may also collect messages you send us through contact forms or support channels.

Store and review data we read

To provide the service, Konfident reads your store's publicly available product review data — the same reviews already displayed to shoppers on your storefront — from your existing review platform (for example Yotpo, Judge.me, Okendo, Stamped, or Loox) and from your product catalog (product titles, SKUs, and related metadata). We process this content to synthesize a confidence signal per product. On the Pro plan, we additionally process purchase-outcome and return data that you connect, which we anonymize before any aggregation (see Section 4).

Technical and usage data

When you use our website or dashboard, we automatically collect standard technical information such as IP address, browser type, device and operating system, pages viewed, referring URLs, and timestamps. We use this to operate, secure, and improve the service, diagnose problems, and understand aggregate usage patterns.

Cookies and similar technologies

We use a small number of cookies and similar technologies on our website and dashboard for essential functionality, security, preferences, and privacy-respecting analytics. See Section 5 for details.

3. How We Use Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Provide, operate, and maintain the Konfident service, including reading review data and generating confidence signals for your product pages;
  • Create and manage your account, authenticate you, and provide customer support;
  • Process payments, manage subscriptions and free trials, and send billing-related communications;
  • Monitor, secure, debug, and improve the service, and develop new features;
  • Communicate with you about product updates, service notices, and — where permitted — occasional marketing you can opt out of;
  • Comply with legal obligations and enforce our Terms of Service.

Where the law requires a legal basis for processing (for example under the GDPR), we rely on the performance of our contract with you, our legitimate interests in operating and improving the service, your consent (for optional cookies and marketing), and compliance with legal obligations.

4. Data We Do NOT Collect

Our approach is defined as much by what we do not do as by what we do. Specifically:

  • We do not collect or store shopper personal information (PII). Konfident does not ask for, receive, or retain the names, email addresses, phone numbers, shipping addresses, payment details, or account identifiers of your shoppers.
  • We read only public review content. The reviews we process are already public on your storefront. We do not scrape private customer records or reviewer contact details.
  • We anonymize Pro return data. Where the Pro plan uses purchase-outcome and return signals, that data is anonymized before it is aggregated so that it cannot reasonably be tied back to an individual shopper.
  • We do not sell personal information. We do not sell or rent personal data to third parties, and we do not build advertising profiles of your shoppers.

5. Cookies & Tracking

We aim to keep our use of cookies minimal and purposeful. We use:

  • Essential cookies — required to log you in, keep your session secure, and remember basic preferences. These cannot be switched off without breaking core functionality.
  • Analytics cookies — used to understand aggregate, privacy-respecting usage of our website and dashboard so we can improve them. Where required by law, we request your consent before setting non-essential analytics cookies.

We do not use cross-site advertising trackers on our product surfaces. You can control cookies through your browser settings; disabling essential cookies may prevent parts of the service from working. Our confidence-signal widget shown on your storefront is designed to operate without setting shopper-identifying cookies.

6. Data Sharing & Sub-processors

We do not sell your data. We share information only as necessary to run the service, and only with vetted providers under appropriate contractual protections. Categories of sub-processors include:

  • Cloud infrastructure and hosting providers that store and run the application;
  • Payment processing providers that handle subscription billing (we do not store full card numbers ourselves);
  • Email and communication providers used for transactional and support messages;
  • Analytics and error-monitoring providers used to keep the service reliable.

We may also disclose information if required by law, to protect our rights and the safety of our users, or in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets — in which case we will notify affected merchants. A current list of sub-processors is available on request by emailing hello@konfident.app.

7. Data Retention

We retain merchant account information for as long as your account is active and as needed to provide the service. Review-derived signals and synthesized data are retained while your store is connected and are refreshed as your reviews change. If you close your account, we delete or de-identify merchant account data within a commercially reasonable period, except where we must retain certain records to meet legal, tax, accounting, or security obligations. Anonymized and aggregated data, which cannot be tied back to an individual, may be retained to improve the service.

8. Security

We take reasonable and appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect the information we handle, including encryption in transit and at rest, least-privilege access controls, and a data-minimal architecture that avoids collecting shopper PII in the first place. No system can be guaranteed perfectly secure, but security is a core design priority. For a fuller, honest overview of our early-stage security posture, see our Security page.

9. Your Rights

We build to be GDPR compliant by design and CCPA ready. Depending on where you live, you may have rights over your personal information, including the right to:

  • Access — request a copy of the personal information we hold about you;
  • Rectification — ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete information;
  • Deletion — ask us to delete your personal information, subject to legal exceptions;
  • Portability — receive certain information in a structured, machine-readable format;
  • Opt-out — opt out of marketing communications and, where applicable, of the "sale" or "sharing" of personal information (note: we do not sell personal information);
  • Object or restrict — object to or restrict certain processing based on your particular situation.

Because Konfident does not collect shopper PII, most personal information we hold relates to merchant accounts. To exercise any right, email hello@konfident.app. We will respond within the timeframes required by applicable law and will not discriminate against you for exercising your rights. If you are in the EU/EEA or UK, you also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority.

10. International Transfers

Konfident may process and store information in countries other than the one in which you are located, including the United States. Where we transfer personal data internationally, we rely on appropriate safeguards recognized under applicable law — such as Standard Contractual Clauses — to protect that data. By using the service, you understand that your information may be transferred to and processed in jurisdictions with different data-protection laws than your own.

11. Children's Privacy

Konfident is a business-to-business tool intended for merchants and store operators. It is not directed to children, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under the age of 16. If you believe a child has provided us personal information, please contact us and we will delete it.

12. Changes to This Policy

As an early-stage company, our practices and this policy will evolve. We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we make material changes, we will update the "Last updated" date above and, where appropriate, notify you by email or through the dashboard. Your continued use of the service after an update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

13. Contact

If you have questions, requests, or concerns about this Privacy Policy or your data, contact us at hello@konfident.app. We aim to respond promptly to every privacy inquiry.
